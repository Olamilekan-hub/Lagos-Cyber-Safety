Phishing Awareness

by Janet on July 25, 2024

Phishing is one of the most common ways cybercriminals try to steal your information. It's important to know how to recognize and avoid phishing scams to protect yourself. Here are some simple tips to help you stay safe.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is when someone tries to trick you into giving them your personal information, like your bank details or passwords. They often do this by sending fake emails or messages that look real.

How to Spot Phishing Scams

  • Look at the Sender's Email Address

    • If you get an email from someone you don't know, check the sender's email address. If it looks strange or doesn't match the company it claims to be from, it might be a phishing scam.

  • Beware of Urgent Messages

    • Phishing emails often try to scare you with urgent messages. They might say your account will be closed if you don't respond quickly. Be cautious of such messages.

  • Check for Spelling and Grammar Mistakes

    • Many phishing emails have spelling and grammar mistakes. If the email looks unprofessional, it could be a scam.

  • Don't Click on Suspicious Links

    • If an email asks you to click a link to update your account or verify your information, be careful. Hover over the link to see where it really goes. If it doesn't look right, don't click it.

  • Don't Share Personal Information

    • Legitimate companies will never ask you for your password or bank details through email. If someone asks for this information, it's probably a scam.

    5. Be Wary of Phishing Scams

    Phishing scams are fake emails or messages that look real but are designed to steal your information. Don’t click on links or download attachments from unknown sources. If something looks suspicious, delete it.

    6. Secure Your Wi-Fi

    Make sure your home Wi-Fi network is secure. Use a strong password and don’t share it with too many people. Turn off your Wi-Fi when you’re not using it.

    7. Backup Your Data

    Regularly backup your important files to an external hard drive or cloud storage. This way, if you are hacked or your device is infected with malware, you won’t lose your important data.

    8. Educate Yourself and Others

    Learn about the different types of cyber threats and how to avoid them. Share this knowledge with your friends and family so they can also stay safe online.

    Conclusion

    Phishing scams are a big problem, but you can protect yourself by staying informed and cautious. By following these simple tips, you can keep your personal information safe and avoid becoming a victim of cybercrime in Lagos.
